Key Biscayne / 64th Annual Fourth of July
The Brief
01

The most prestigious Fourth of July parade in Florida needed a visual identity worthy of it.

Key Biscayne is the southernmost incorporated village in the continental United States — and arguably the most exclusive. The annual Fourth of July parade is a civic institution: 64 editions deep, beloved by residents who live on one of the most valuable stretches of real estate in the country. The brief was to create a visual identity system for the 64th edition that honored that legacy without feeling stiff.

Working with Rocketdog Creative Studio, the direction landed on tarot — a surprising reference that worked immediately. The tarot card format gave us a container for illustration and lettering that felt ceremonial, collectible, and distinctly un-corporate. The visual vocabulary drew from the village's iconic lighthouse and the tropical blooms that frame it: dramatic, lush, unmistakably Key Biscayne.

Process
02
01
Finding the Right Register
Key Biscayne is not the kind of client that wants loud. The visual language needed to communicate prestige and celebration simultaneously — without tipping into either generic patriotic kitsch or cold luxury minimalism. The tarot card format resolved that tension: it's ceremonial by nature, illustrative by tradition, and rich enough to carry the detail the parade deserved.
02
Lighthouse and Tropical Blooms
The Cape Florida Lighthouse is the defining landmark of the village — the oldest structure in Miami-Dade County, standing since 1825. It anchors the composition. Surrounding it: the dense, specific tropical flora of the island — heliconias, sea grapes, bougainvillea. The illustration was built in layers, dense enough to reward close inspection, bold enough to read at parade scale.
03
Custom Lettering as the Voice
The lettering composition carried the edition number — 64th Annual — with the same weight as the illustration. Hand-lettered numerals, a display script for the parade name, and structured serif type for supporting information. Everything calibrated to feel handcrafted and official at the same time. Legible at poster size, luxurious at close range.
04
Across Every Surface
The system extended beyond the hero illustration: beach bags, street signage, framed poster editions, and parade collateral. The tarot card as an object became collectible — something residents would keep. That was the intent: not just event branding, but a commemorative artifact for a community that takes its traditions seriously.
