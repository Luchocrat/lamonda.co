Proper Roots / Brand Identity
The Brief
01

Everyone had a vision. The job was to focus them into something their audience would actually buy.

Proper Roots came in with a motif, a name, and five stakeholders with five different ideas of what the brand should be. The challenge wasn't design — it was alignment. Getting everyone to agree on what would resonate with their customer before a single label was printed.

The solution started with 5ml concentrate jars in 12 flavors in early 2025. A focused first drop that proved the system before it scaled. Raised Spot UV on the roots motif was the defining material decision — premium without being ornate, tactile before it's readable. Everything else followed from that one call.

Process
02
01
Stakeholder Alignment
The brief was as much facilitation as design. Five stakeholders, multiple visions, one brand. The process started with understanding what each person was protecting — then finding the overlap that would actually speak to the customer. That alignment became the foundation every design decision was built on.
02
Material Decision First
Raised Spot UV on the roots motif wasn't an aesthetic choice — it was a strategic one. It set the register for the entire line: premium, tactile, considered. A brand you feel before you read. That one decision cascaded into every format that followed.
03
Building the Format System
Starting with 5ml concentrate jars, the system was built to scale without losing its integrity. Each new format — 3.5g and 7g flower jars, tuck-boxes, pre-rolls, 1g flower pop-tops — inherited the same visual rules. Every shipper got its own colorway. The roots motif held across all of it.
04
The Slapwood Hash-Hole Collab
The 2g hash-hole SKU became a collaboration with Slapwood Papers. A collab is a test of any system — does it have enough range to host a different voice without losing itself? The Slapwood drop answered that. Same roots, different register. Proof the brand had depth.
