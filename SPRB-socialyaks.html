Give kids from rough upbringings a safe space to talk through it.

Social Yaks is a card game designed as a community outreach tool — specifically for young people from difficult backgrounds who need a structured, safe way to talk about what they've been through.

The brief was to give this idea a visual identity that matched the mission: character-driven, warm but not naive, with illustration and custom lettering that young people could connect with without feeling like they were being handled.

Process
04
01
Characters as Conversation Starters
Each animal in the deck was designed to carry emotional weight — not just look interesting. The expressions had to communicate openness and safety before a single question was read.
02
Lettering as Brand Voice
Custom lettering was used to brand the game itself — not a typeface, actual hand-crafted type. This gave Social Yaks a voice that felt personal without feeling childish.
03
The Animal Hierarchy
The animal hierarchy wasn't decorative — it was functional. Different animals signal different depths of question. Players understand the emotional stakes before they flip a card.
04
Couples Edition Expansion
The Couples Edition grew naturally from the original system. A new set of cards tailored specifically to relationship challenges, using the same character-driven framework in a new emotional context.
