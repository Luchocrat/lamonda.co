The best icebreaker is one that doesn't feel like one.

Social Yaks is a community card game designed to create real conversation. The brief: build a visual world around a yak — an animal expressive enough to carry any emotion, absurd enough to disarm any tension.

The result: a full character illustration system, card design architecture, and a game identity that feels like a cult object from the first time you hold it.

Process
01
Character Development
The yak needed to carry every human emotion without being human. Dozens of expressions sketched before locking the final character system. Each card gets a unique expression.
02
Card Architecture
Three card types, one visual language. The hierarchy had to be legible in two seconds across a table. Color-coded by type, illustrated by character.
03
Game Identity
The brand extends beyond the cards: box design, instruction booklet, social identity. Every touchpoint reinforces the absurdist warmth of the brand.
04
Print Production
Printed to playing-card spec. Linen finish. Spot UV on the box. The physical object had to feel as considered as the design.
