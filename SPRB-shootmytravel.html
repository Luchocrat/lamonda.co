The users knew what was wrong. The job was to turn that into a roadmap.

Shoot My Travel is a photography booking platform connecting travelers with local photographers in destinations around the world. The product worked — but users were dropping off and photographers weren't getting enough visibility.

The engagement started with a listening exercise: direct inquiry to users on where the site was creating friction, and what would make them book faster or more often. The answers became the brief.

Process
04
01
User Pain Point Mapping
Before a single wireframe, we talked to users. Direct inquiry — where are you losing momentum? What's stopping you from booking? The answers revealed patterns no analytics dashboard would show.
02
Dashboard Reassessment
The photographer dashboard was the core of the platform's supply side — if photographers couldn't manage their visibility, travelers couldn't find them. It needed a complete rethink.
03
Turning Friction into Opportunity
Every pain point had a stakeholder opportunity behind it. Slow discovery became better search and filter logic. Weak trust signals became a clearer photographer profile system.
04
Communication and Ops Improvements
Some of the highest-impact changes weren't UI changes at all — they were communication and operations recommendations. Automated follow-ups, clearer booking confirmation, better response time expectations.
