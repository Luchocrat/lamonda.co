The Brief
01

Brands built on a person need to carry that person's weight.

Triple Crown is Jason Werth's brand. Former Major League Baseball. A career built on precision, pressure, and performance. The identity had to carry all of that before it carried anything else.

Two designers. Thirteen options. Nothing landed. The brand arrived needing a foundation, not a revision. The ask was a graphic identity worthy of the name. From scratch.

Process
02
01
Direction Before Drawing
Thirteen failed options tell you the problem is not execution, it is direction. Before a single mark was drawn, the work was understanding what Triple Crown needed to stand for. Not what it needed to look like.
02
Building the Mark
No borrowed elements. No shortcuts. The logo was built from the ground up: a graphic identity carrying the heritage of baseball, the precision of premium branding, and the identity of a founder who built something on his own terms.
03
System and Launch
The identity launched with two initial SKUs. Enough to establish the visual language and prove the system before expansion. Every application decision reinforced the premium positioning the brand required.
04
The Black Line
Currently in development: the Black Line. A premium top-tier strain in glass jars for 3.5g and 7g. The identity built for day one is the identity scaling into year two. Getting the foundation right is not a detail. It is the whole job.
